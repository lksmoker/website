# Task Summary - PM Hiring Appeal Review

## Task
Evaluate the website repository, with emphasis on `src/content/*`, for realistic appeal to hiring managers for Product Manager roles.

## Scope Reviewed
- `src/content/home.json`
- `src/content/about.json`
- `src/content/work.json`
- `src/content/projects.json`
- `src/content/writing.json`
- `src/content/aurora.json`
- `src/content/contact.json`
- Light context: `README.md`

## Overall Assessment
**Current strength: 6.5/10 (good narrative, moderate hiring conversion risk).**

The content communicates strong systems thinking, ownership language, and cross-domain leadership. It reads thoughtful and differentiated. However, for PM hiring managers, it is currently too abstract in places and under-indexed on hard business evidence (metrics, outcomes, scope, prioritization artifacts, and explicit PM operating mechanisms).

## What Is Strong
- Clear throughline: systems clarity, feedback loops, explainability, revision.
- Credible ownership framing in project entries (problem, decisions, tradeoffs).
- Real operations background creates differentiated PM story.
- Structured case-study style in `projects.json` is directionally strong.

## What Lowers PM Hiring Confidence
- Too much philosophy vs. measurable product impact.
  - Multiple sections emphasize concepts without concrete KPIs or before/after data.
- Missing/weak PM proof points.
  - Limited evidence of roadmap prioritization frameworks, stakeholder alignment methods, experimentation, adoption metrics, or business outcomes.
- Some entries are vague or internally inconsistent.
  - Example: `gas-transportation-workflow` uses `timeline: "Grace College"` (organization, not timeline).
  - `writing.json` article `date` values are blank.
- “Aurora” is interesting but can read as speculative unless tied to shipped outcomes and PM decisions.
- Several claims would be stronger with explicit scale/scope/context (team size, users, cycle time reduction, error-rate change, cost/time savings).

## Hiring-Manager Realism Verdict
A hiring manager will likely see a strong thinker/operator with builder capability, but may hesitate at interview-screen stage due to limited quant proof and limited explicit PM execution artifacts. With tighter outcome evidence and PM-specific operating detail, this can move into a clearly strong profile.

## Highest-Impact Content Improvements
1. Add measurable outcomes to each featured project.
2. Add a short “PM Operating Model” section with concrete methods (prioritization, discovery, stakeholder cadence, decision logs).
3. Reframe conceptual language to business language in hero/intro copy.
4. Normalize timelines/dates and remove blanks.
5. Add one compact “selected metrics” block near top-level pages (`home`, `work`).
