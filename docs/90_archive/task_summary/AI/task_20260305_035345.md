## PM Hiring Appeal Assessment
Overall strength: **6.5/10**.

This portfolio reads thoughtful and differentiated, with strong systems thinking and clear ownership language. However, for PM hiring managers, conversion risk is moderate because too much copy is conceptual and not enough is measurable execution evidence.

## What Is Strong
1. Clear throughline across pages: systems clarity, feedback loops, and explainable decisions.
2. Strong ownership framing in project entries (problem, decisions, tradeoffs).
3. Differentiated background (operations plus software building) that can be compelling for PM roles in complex environments.
4. Work page structure is generally good for case-study style review.

## What Lowers Confidence For PM Screeners
1. Limited hard outcomes. Many claims are not tied to KPI deltas, adoption, cycle time, revenue/cost impact, or baseline vs after.
2. PM operating mechanics are under-specified (prioritization method, discovery process, stakeholder management cadence, experiment design, roadmap tradeoffs).
3. Some data quality issues reduce credibility:
   - `projects.json` -> `gas-transportation-workflow` has `timeline: "Grace College"` (organization, not time).
   - `writing.json` article `date` fields are blank.
4. Aurora content is interesting but can read as speculative unless repeatedly tied to shipped outcomes and business impact.
5. Several sections use broad language where hiring managers expect concrete proof.

## Hiring Manager Realism Verdict
A hiring manager will likely see strong product thinking and execution potential, but may hesitate before interview unless evidence is tightened. With quantified outcomes and explicit PM process detail, this can move to a clearly strong PM profile.

## Highest-Impact Improvements
1. Add measurable impact bullets to each featured project.
2. Add a short PM operating model section (discovery, prioritization, decision logs, stakeholder alignment).
3. Replace some abstract copy in hero/intro with business-value statements.
4. Normalize timeline/date fields and remove blanks.
5. Add a compact "selected metrics" block on Home and Work.
